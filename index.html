About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at University College Dublin, Ireland, working at the intersection of ubiquitous computing, human activity sensing, and sustainable transport. My research integrates wearable sensors, machine learning, and data analytics to address real-world challenges in health, mobility, and environmental sustainability. I am passionate about applied research and enjoy building proof-of-concept systems that translate technical innovations into practical solutions.

Currently, I am part of the Research Ireland-funded SHIFT project, where we investigate cyclist stress using physiological signals to evaluate cycling infrastructure and understand its impact on comfort and safety during active travel.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science & Engineering at IIIT-Delhi, under the guidance of Prof. Vinayak Naik and Dr. Dipanjan Chakraborty. I also hold an M.Tech with a specialization in Information Security.
